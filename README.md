# ThinkFirst — Smarter AI Usage Starts With Independent Thinking

ThinkFirst is an Android educational application designed to reduce unhealthy AI dependency in computer science learning.
Instead of giving students instant answers, the app encourages them to attempt problems independently first, reflect on their thinking, and then use AI more critically and effectively.

The core idea is simple:

> AI should support thinking — not replace it.

Students write or speak their own attempt before hints become available.
The more effort they put in, the more guidance the system unlocks.

---

# Core Learning Philosophy

Most AI tools optimize for fast answers.

ThinkFirst optimizes for:

* independent problem solving
* critical thinking
* reflection
* AI evaluation skills
* long-term retention

The app transforms AI from a shortcut into a guided learning partner.

---

# Main Features

## Progressive Hint System

Hints are locked behind effort thresholds.

Students must first:

* type an answer
* or speak their thoughts using voice input

Three hint tiers are unlocked progressively:

| Effort     | Hint Type     |
| ---------- | ------------- |
| 10+ chars  | Gentle nudge  |
| 30+ chars  | Partial hint  |
| 150+ chars | Full guidance |

This encourages active thinking before assistance.

---

## AI Mirror

After solving independently, students can compare their answer with an AI-generated explanation.

The system highlights:

* what the student covered
* what the AI covered
* missing concepts
* overlapping understanding

This turns AI into a reflection tool instead of an answer machine.

---

## Detective Mode — “CatchTheAI”

A gamified mode where students analyze intentionally flawed AI responses.

Students must:

* detect hallucinations
* flag misleading statements
* identify fabricated information
* judge correctness

This trains critical evaluation of AI output rather than blind trust.

---

## Voice-to-Text Learning

Students can speak their reasoning using Android’s SpeechRecognizer API.

Features include:

* live transcription
* animated waveform feedback
* effort tracking from spoken answers

---

## Thinking Independence System

The app includes a visual “Thinking Tank” that changes depending on learning behavior.

Independent work increases the tank.
Heavy hint usage reduces it.

This creates a long-term motivation system focused on self-reliance.

---

## Cold Open Recall

After returning to a subject after 48+ hours, the app asks students to recall a previously solved concept from memory before continuing.

This reinforces retention and active recall learning.

# Application Workflow

## 1. Home Screen

Students choose a subject and view:

* progress
* rank
* thinking independence level
* recent activity

---

## 2. Chapter Selection

Each subject contains multiple learning tasks.

Completed chapters display:

* completion badges
* effort indicators
* retake options
* Detective Mode unlock progress

---

## 3. Task Attempt

Students:

* answer by typing or speaking
* build effort progressively
* unlock hints step-by-step
* receive concept-based feedback

---

## 4. Hint Reflection

Hints are shown gradually with:

* concept coverage chips
* reflection prompts
* guided support instead of direct solutions

---

## 5. Submission & Reveal

After submission:

* effort is analyzed
* concepts are checked
* progress is saved
* independence score is updated

Independent submissions unlock AI Mirror comparison.

---

## 6. Detective Mode

Students investigate flawed AI answers through:

* Hunt Mode
* Jury Mode
* Verdict Review

Scores and ranks are awarded based on accuracy.

# Educational Goal

ThinkFirst is not designed to compete with AI tools.

It is designed to teach students:

* how to think before asking AI
* how to question AI responses
* how to collaborate with AI responsibly
* how to remain intellectually independent
